Sustainability

It's defined as the avoidance of the depletion of natural resources in order to maintain an ecological balance. But according to the UN's Sustainable Development Goals, sustainability can mean a lot more!

    Impact on Wildlife

    According to the United Nations, at least 800 species worldwide are affected by marine debris, and as much as 80 percent of that litter is plastic. It is estimated that up to 13 million metric tons of plastic ends up in the ocean each year—the equivalent of a rubbish or garbage truck load’s worth every minute. Fish, seabirds, sea turtles, and marine mammals can become entangled in or ingest plastic debris, causing suffocation, starvation, and drowning. So it’s little wonder that it’s predicted there will be a higher volume of plastic in the oceans than fish by 2050.

    Pollution

    Every day, we pollute the world with our waste. Maybe you litter, drive, or buy clothing from a company that cares more for profits than for the environment. But every year, 100 million mammals die from this pollution. Every year, 14 billion pounds of our pollution is dumped into the ocean.

    Zero waste lifestyle

    Zero Waste is a movement to reduce the amount one consumes and consequently throws away. Adopting a Zero Waste lifestyle is one of the most sustainable ways of living. Zero Waste lifestyle choices influence all environmental areas by preventing resource extraction, reducing the amount of materials sent to the landfill or incinerator, and reducing pollution from producing, transporting, or disposing of materials.
